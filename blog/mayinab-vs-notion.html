The Problem: Notes in One App, Flashcards in Another

If you're a student, you probably know this workflow: take notes in Notion, then copy your key points into Anki or Quizlet to create flashcards. Two apps, double the work, and the constant friction of switching between them.

Notion is a fantastic productivity tool: wikis, databases, project boards, shared workspaces. But it was never built for studying. It has no flashcards, no spaced repetition, no review scheduling, and its offline support is limited.

Mayinab was designed from the ground up as a study-first app. Notes and flashcards live together in the same timeline. Built-in spaced repetition (SM-2) schedules your reviews automatically. No copy-pasting, no third-party integrations, no internet required.

Notion Wasn't Built for Active Recall

Notion is incredible for organizing information: creating wikis, databases, project boards, and shared workspaces. But when it comes to actually learning that information, Notion falls short.

The science is clear: active recall and spaced repetition are the two most effective study techniques. You need to test yourself with flashcards and space your reviews over increasing intervals. Notion has neither of these features built in.

Students who use Notion for studying end up re-reading their notes passively, one of the least effective study methods according to research. To use active recall with Notion, you need external plugins or must manually create toggle blocks as makeshift flashcards, with no scheduling.

Mayinab: Notes and Flashcards in One Place

In Mayinab, your study flow is seamless. Take a text note about a topic, then type /flashcard right below it to create a card. The flashcard lives inline with your notes in a chat-style timeline. No switching apps, no copy-pasting.

When it's time to review, Mayinab's SM-2 spaced repetition algorithm schedules your cards automatically. You get flip mode or typing mode, streak tracking, and daily reminders. Your learning is powered by science, not just note storage.

Offline: A Critical Difference

Notion is cloud-first. Your data lives on Notion's servers. While it caches recently viewed pages for offline access, you can't create new pages, search across your workspace, or access content you haven't recently opened. None of this works without internet.

Mayinab is local-first. Everything is stored on your device. It works in airplane mode, in the library basement, on a train with no signal. You never need internet to study. Optional encrypted cloud backup (AES-256) is available but never required.

For students who study in places with unreliable internet, such as university libraries, commutes, or travel, this is a game-changer.

Privacy: Your Notes, Your Device

With Notion, your notes are stored on their cloud servers. They have access to your data, and their privacy policy allows data processing for various purposes. If Notion goes down, your notes are temporarily inaccessible.

Mayinab takes a fundamentally different approach: your data never leaves your device. No account required, no tracking, no analytics. If you choose cloud backup, it's encrypted with AES-256 before leaving your device; even Mayinab can't read it.

7 Note Types vs. Notion Blocks

Notion's block system is incredibly flexible: text, headings, toggles, tables, databases, embeds, and more. It's a general-purpose workspace that can be bent into almost anything.

Mayinab takes a focused approach with 7 purpose-built note types for studying:

  • Text notes: Markdown + LaTeX, wiki-links, #tags
  • Flashcards: flip and typing modes, SM-2 scheduling
  • Checklists: track study goals and tasks
  • Image notes: with built-in scribble pad for annotations
  • Audio notes: record or attach lectures
  • Video notes: embed video references
  • File attachments: drag & drop PDFs, slides, documents

Everything is organized in a Subject ‚Üí Topic hierarchy with unlimited nesting. Less flexible than Notion's databases, but laser-focused on what students actually need.

Desktop Experience

Both apps offer strong desktop experiences, but with different philosophies:

  • Mayinab: Resizable split-view, Discord-style sidebar, command palette (Ctrl+K), full keyboard shortcuts, custom themes with wallpapers
  • Notion: Clean page-based layout, side peek panels, breadcrumb navigation, team collaboration features

If you need a team workspace with databases, Notion wins. If you need a personal study station with flashcards and SRS, Mayinab is purpose-built for that.
