Stop Using Two Apps to Study. Combine Notes and Flashcards in One.

The Two-App Problem

Most students study with at least two apps: one for note-taking (Notion, OneNote, Google Docs) and another for flashcards (Anki, Quizlet, Brainscape). This creates a fragmented workflow:

  1. Take notes in App A during class
  2. After class, manually copy key concepts into App B as flashcards
  3. Study flashcards in App B separately
  4. Repeat for every subject, every week

The copy-paste step is where most students give up. It's tedious, time-consuming, and breaks the flow between learning and reviewing. Research shows that making flashcards close to the source material improves retention, but the friction of switching apps often prevents this.

Why Notes and Flashcards Should Live Together

The science of learning is clear on two points:

  • Active recall: testing yourself is 3x more effective than re-reading notes
  • Spaced repetition: reviewing at increasing intervals prevents forgetting

When your notes and flashcards are in the same app, you naturally create cards while taking notes. This means:

  • No "I'll make flashcards later" procrastination
  • Cards are created in context, leading to better quality questions
  • Your notes serve as study reference alongside your flashcards
  • One organized system instead of scattered tools

Current Options (and Why They Fall Short)

Notion + Anki

Notion is excellent for note-taking but has zero flashcard or spaced repetition support. Anki has powerful SRS but no note-taking beyond basic card fields. Using both means constant context-switching and manual copying.

Quizlet

Quizlet has flashcards but its note-taking is minimal. It's cloud-dependent, has ads on the free tier, and its spaced repetition is less sophisticated than SM-2. It also locks key features behind a paid plan.

RemNote / Obsidian + Plugins

These tools attempt to bridge the gap with plugins or built-in features, but they often feel bolted on. The learning curve is steep, and flashcard creation isn't as frictionless as it should be.
