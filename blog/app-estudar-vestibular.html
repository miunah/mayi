What Are Vestibulares?

Vestibulares are Brazil's university entrance exams, administered independently by each institution. Unlike ENEM (Brazil's national exam), vestibulares often have two phases, include essay questions, and test deeper, more specific knowledge.

The most competitive vestibulares include FUVEST (USP), UNICAMP (Comvest), UNESP (Vunesp), UERJ, UFMG, UFRGS, and many others. Each has its own style, required reading lists, and content emphasis.

Preparing for vestibulares requires deep understanding of subjects and long-term retention of hundreds of concepts. That's where spaced repetition and Mayinab come in.

Why Vestibulares Are Harder Than ENEM

While ENEM uses multiple-choice questions with a focus on interpretation, vestibulares often require:

  • Discursive answers: You must write full solutions and explanations
  • Deeper content: Topics go beyond the basics covered in ENEM
  • Mandatory reading: Literature lists with specific books you must know in detail
  • Two phases: First phase is broad; second phase is deep and subject-specific
  • Higher competition: Medicine at USP can have 100+ candidates per spot

This means you need a study method that ensures nothing is forgotten while you advance through the syllabus.

How Spaced Repetition Solves the Vestibular Problem

The biggest challenge in vestibular prep is volume. You study Biology in January, and by June you've forgotten half of it because you moved on to Chemistry and Physics.

Spaced repetition with the SM-2 algorithm solves this by scheduling reviews at the optimal moment -- right before you forget. Cards you know well appear less frequently; cards you struggle with appear more often.

Result: you retain all subjects simultaneously without dedicating entire weeks to review.
