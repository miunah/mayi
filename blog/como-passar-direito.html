How to Pass Vestibular de Direito: Complete Study Plan

February 13, 2026 ¬∑ 13 min read

Why the Law Vestibular Demands a Different Approach

Direito (Law) is one of the most competitive courses in Brazil. At USP (FUVEST), the ratio exceeds 30 candidates per spot. At FGV Direito SP, UNICAMP, and PUC-SP, competition is equally intense.

Unlike Medicine, which leans heavily on exact sciences, Direito vestibulares demand deep mastery of Humanities: History, Geography, Philosophy, Sociology, Literature, and above all, exceptional writing skills.

The challenge is that Humanities content is vast and interconnected. You need to retain hundreds of dates, historical movements, philosophical concepts, sociological theories, and literary works -- and articulate them clearly in essays.

The Key Subjects for Direito Vestibulares

1. History: Brazil and World

History is the backbone of the Direito vestibular. Focus on:

  • History of Brazil: Colonial period, Empire, Republic, Era Vargas, Military Dictatorship, Redemocratization
  • World History: French Revolution, Industrial Revolution, World Wars, Cold War, Decolonization
  • Contemporary History: Globalization, rise of China, populism, migration, climate politics
  • Social movements: Abolitionism, feminism, labor movements, civil rights

Create flashcards with dates, causes, consequences, and key figures. For example: "What were the 3 main causes of the French Revolution?" with a detailed answer covering Enlightenment, economic crisis, and social inequality.

2. Geography: Geopolitics and Current Events

Geography for Direito connects directly to law and social issues:

  • Geopolitics: International organizations, trade blocs, territorial conflicts
  • Urban Geography: Urbanization, slums, gentrification, public transportation
  • Agricultural Geography: Agribusiness, agrarian reform, environmental impact
  • Demographics: Population growth, migration, aging population, social indicators
  • Current Events: International conflicts, elections, social policies, environmental law

3. Philosophy and Sociology

Critical for Direito -- these subjects form the theoretical foundation of Law:

  • Philosophy: Socrates, Plato, Aristotle, Kant, Marx, Foucault, Hannah Arendt
  • Political Philosophy: Social contract (Hobbes, Locke, Rousseau), justice, state and power
  • Sociology: Durkheim, Weber, Marx. Social stratification, institutions, deviance
  • Ethics: Human rights, justice theories, utilitarian vs. deontological ethics

Use Mayinab's Markdown notes to write detailed summaries of each philosopher's key ideas, then create flashcards for the specific concepts you need to recall.

4. Portuguese and Literature

Direito demands impeccable command of the Portuguese language:

  • Grammar: Verb agreement, pronoun placement, rhetorical figures, cohesion
  • Text Interpretation: Critical reading, identifying arguments, authorial intent
  • Literature: Mandatory reading (FUVEST/UNICAMP), literary movements, key authors
  • Redacao (Essay): Argumentative structure, repertoire, formal register

5. Redacao (Essay): The Decisive Factor

The essay is often the single most important component of the Direito vestibular. At FUVEST, a poor essay can eliminate even high-scoring candidates.

Use Mayinab to build a repertoire notebook: historical events, philosophical arguments, statistics, and quotes that can support your essays on any theme. Create flashcards for your best repertoire pieces so you never forget them.
