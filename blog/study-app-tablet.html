Split-View: The Tablet Study Advantage

The biggest advantage of studying on a tablet is screen space. Unlike a phone, where you can only see one thing at a time, a tablet lets you use Mayinab's split-view panels to work with two panes side by side.

Here's how students use split-view on tablets:

  • Notes + Flashcards: Read your notes on the left, review flashcards on the right
  • Subject + Topic: Browse your subject list on the left, read topic content on the right
  • Reference + Notes: Open an image or PDF attachment on one side, take notes on the other
  • Review + Progress: See your review session on one pane and your streak dashboard on the other

The panels are resizable. Drag the divider to give more space to whichever pane you're focusing on. This mirrors the desktop experience, making your tablet feel like a proper study workstation.

Comfortable Flashcard Reviews on a Big Screen

Reviewing flashcards on a phone can feel cramped: small text, tiny buttons, constant scrolling. On a tablet, Mayinab's flashcard review mode uses the full screen width for large, readable cards.

You get two review modes:

  • Flip mode: See the question, tap to reveal the answer, rate your recall (Again, Hard, Good, Easy)
  • Typing mode: Type your answer before revealing the correct one for deeper active recall

The SM-2 spaced repetition algorithm schedules your next review based on how well you remembered each card. Cards you struggle with appear more frequently; cards you know well are spaced out over days and weeks. This is the same algorithm used by Anki, backed by decades of cognitive science research.

On a tablet, the larger buttons and more spacious layout make reviewing sessions faster and less fatiguing. You can review 50-100 cards comfortably in one sitting.
