The Vocabulary Problem: Why Most Language Learners Plateau

Every language learner hits the same wall. You study for weeks, learn hundreds of words, feel like you're making progress... then one day you realize you've forgotten half of what you learned. You go back, re-learn it, and the cycle repeats.

This isn't a motivation problem. It's a memory problem. The forgetting curve, discovered by Herman Ebbinghaus in 1885, shows that we forget up to 70% of new information within 24 hours. Without a system to fight this, language study becomes an endless loop of learning and forgetting.

Spaced repetition is the solution. By reviewing vocabulary at scientifically optimal intervals, you transfer words from short-term to long-term memory efficiently. And the best tool for this is a flashcard app with SM-2 algorithm support.

How Many Words Do You Need?

Research in applied linguistics provides clear benchmarks for vocabulary size:

  • 250 words: Basic survival (greetings, numbers, common verbs)
  • 500 words: Simple conversations about daily life
  • 2,000 words: ~80% comprehension of everyday spoken language
  • 5,000 words: ~95% comprehension, comfortable conversational fluency
  • 10,000+ words: Near-native comprehension, academic and professional fluency

Memorizing 2,000-5,000 words sounds daunting, but with spaced repetition and 15-20 new cards per day, you can reach 2,000 words in about 4-5 months and 5,000 in about 10-12 months. The SM-2 algorithm ensures you don't forget old words while adding new ones.

Why Flashcards + Spaced Repetition Work for Languages

Language learning combines two challenges flashcards are perfect for:

1. Massive Volume of Discrete Facts

Vocabulary words, verb conjugations, grammar rules, idiomatic expressions: these are individual facts that need to be memorized. Flashcards break them into atomic units, one concept per card, which is the most efficient format for memory.

2. Long-Term Retention Required

Unlike studying for a test (where you need to remember for one day), language learning requires you to remember vocabulary for years. Spaced repetition is the only study technique proven to produce this kind of long-term retention.

3. Active Recall Over Passive Review

Reading a vocabulary list and thinking "I know this" doesn't work. Flashcards force you to actively retrieve the translation from memory before checking. This retrieval practice is 3x more effective than passive review.

How to Study Any Language with Mayinab

Step 1: Create Language-Specific Notebooks

Organize your study material into notebooks by topic or level:

  • Basics: Greetings, numbers, colors, days, months

  • Travel: Directions, transportation, hotel, restaurant
  • Grammar: Verb conjugations, tenses, prepositions, cases
  • Advanced: Idioms, formal language, specialized vocabulary

Step 2: Build Effective Vocabulary Flashcards

The best vocabulary flashcard format:

  • Front: Target language word (e.g., "Bibliothek")
  • Back: Translation + gender/article + example sentence (e.g., "Library (die Bibliothek). Ich gehe in die Bibliothek. = I go to the library.")

Tips for better cards:

  • One word per card. Don't put multiple words on a single card.
  • Include context. An example sentence makes the word 2x easier to remember.
  • Add grammatical info. Gender, article, plural form, verb group.
  • Use both directions. Create cards in both directions (target ‚Üí native and native ‚Üí target) for production and recognition.

Step 3: Add Grammar Cards

Grammar is more than rules: it's patterns. Create flashcards for:

  • Verb conjugation tables: "How do you conjugate 'ser' in the present tense?"
  • Grammar rules: "When do you use subjunctive in Spanish?"
  • Sentence patterns: "How do you form a question in German?"
  • Exception lists: "What are the irregular past participles in French?"

Step 4: Take Notes Alongside Flashcards

This is where Mayinab truly shines. Instead of using one app for notes (Notion, Google Docs) and another for flashcards (Anki, Quizlet), you can do both in the same timeline. Write a note explaining a grammar concept, then create flashcards right below it using /flashcard.

Step 5: Review Daily, No Exceptions

Spaced repetition only works with daily consistency. The SM-2 algorithm schedules cards at optimal intervals, but skipping days causes review pile-up. Aim for:

  • 15-20 minutes daily for review
  • 10-20 new cards per day (don't go higher)
  • Morning review is most effective (fresh brain)

10 Tips for Learning Any Language

  1. Start with the 500 most frequent words. They cover ~70% of everyday conversation. Use a frequency list for your target language.
  2. Learn words in sentences, not isolation. Context makes vocabulary 2x easier to remember. Always include example sentences in flashcards.
  3. Create cards in both directions. Word ‚Üí Translation (recognition) and Translation ‚Üí Word (production). You need both skills.
  4. Study grammar as patterns, not rules. Instead of memorizing abstract rules, create flashcards with example sentences that demonstrate the pattern.
  5. Review every single day. Even 10 minutes counts. Skipping days causes review pile-up and reduces effectiveness.
  6. Limit new cards to 15-20 per day. Adding too many causes unsustainable review loads. Slow and steady wins the language race.
  7. Use your language outside flashcards. Watch shows, listen to podcasts, read graded readers, speak with native speakers. Flashcards build knowledge; immersion builds fluency.
  8. Don't chase perfection. Marking a card "good" when you remembered 80% is fine. Progress matters more than perfection.
  9. Include pronunciation info. For languages with different scripts (Korean, Arabic, Russian), include romanization on your cards initially.
  10. Trust the algorithm. Don't manually review cards before they're due. The SM-2 algorithm knows when you need to see something again.

O Problema do Vocabulario: Por que a Maioria dos Estudantes de Idiomas Estagna

Todo estudante de idiomas esbarra no mesmo muro. Voce estuda por semanas, aprende centenas de palavras, sente que esta progredindo... e um dia percebe que esqueceu metade do que aprendeu. Volta, re-aprende, e o ciclo repete.

Nao e um problema de motivacao. E um problema de memoria. A curva do esquecimento, descoberta por Hermann Ebbinghaus em 1885, mostra que esquecemos ate 70% de informacoes novas em 24 horas. Sem um sistema para combater isso, o estudo de idiomas vira um loop infinito de aprender e esquecer.

Repeticao espacada e a solucao. Ao revisar vocabulario em intervalos cientificamente otimizados, voce transfere palavras da memoria de curto prazo para a de longo prazo de forma eficiente. E a melhor ferramenta para isso e um app de flashcards com suporte ao algoritmo SM-2.

Quantas Palavras Voce Precisa?

Pesquisas em linguistica aplicada fornecem marcos claros para tamanho de vocabulario:

  • 250 palavras: Sobrevivencia basica (cumprimentos, numeros, verbos comuns)
  • 500 palavras: Conversas simples sobre vida cotidiana
  • 2.000 palavras: ~80% de compreensao da lingua falada no dia a dia
  • 5.000 palavras: ~95% de compreensao, fluencia conversacional confortavel
  • 10.000+ palavras: Compreensao quase nativa, fluencia academica e profissional

Memorizar 2.000-5.000 palavras parece assustador, mas com repeticao espacada e 15-20 novos cards por dia, voce pode atingir 2.000 palavras em cerca de 4-5 meses e 5.000 em cerca de 10-12 meses. O algoritmo SM-2 garante que voce nao esqueca palavras antigas enquanto adiciona novas.

Por que Flashcards + Repeticao Espacada Funcionam para Idiomas

O aprendizado de idiomas combina dois desafios para os quais flashcards sao perfeitos:

1. Volume Massivo de Fatos Discretos

Palavras de vocabulario, conjugacoes verbais, regras gramaticais, expressoes idiomaticas: sao fatos individuais que precisam ser memorizados. Flashcards os dividem em unidades atomicas, um conceito por card, que e o formato mais eficiente para memoria.

2. Retencao de Longo Prazo Necessaria

Diferente de estudar para uma prova (onde voce precisa lembrar por um dia), aprender idiomas exige que voce lembre vocabulario por anos. Repeticao espacada e a unica tecnica de estudo comprovada para produzir esse tipo de retencao de longo prazo.

3. Recall Ativo Sobre Revisao Passiva

Ler uma lista de vocabulario e pensar "eu sei isso" nao funciona. Flashcards forcam voce a recuperar ativamente a traducao da memoria antes de conferir. Essa pratica de recuperacao e 3x mais eficaz que revisao passiva.
