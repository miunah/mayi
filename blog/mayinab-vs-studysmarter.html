Quick Overview

StudySmarter is a European study platform that combines notes, flashcards, and shared study materials in a cloud-based app. It's growing fast, especially among university students in Europe and the UK. Mayinab is a modern study app that also merges notes and flashcards, but with SM-2 spaced repetition, a chat-style interface, and a focus on privacy, offline access, and zero cost.

Both are "all-in-one" study apps, but they take very different approaches to data, pricing, and study methodology. Let's compare.

Spaced Repetition: SM-2 vs Basic Scheduling

Spaced repetition is the most effective study technique backed by cognitive science. The quality of the algorithm matters.

Mayinab uses the SM-2 algorithm, the same proven system used by Anki. Each flashcard has an individual ease factor that adapts based on your recall performance. Intervals are calculated mathematically for optimal long-term retention.

StudySmarter has flashcards with a basic spaced repetition mode, but it doesn't use SM-2 or any well-documented algorithm. The scheduling feels more like a simple leitner box system. For quick review, it works. For optimized long-term retention, particularly in demanding fields like medicine, law, or languages, SM-2 delivers measurably better results.

Pricing: Free vs Freemium

Mayinab is 100% free. All features included: spaced repetition, 7 note types, Markdown, LaTeX, offline mode, themes. The only paid option is Vault (encrypted cloud backup) starting at $1.50/month, entirely optional.

StudySmarter uses an aggressive freemium model. The free tier includes ads and limited features. StudySmarter Premium costs ‚Ç¨9.99/month (or ‚Ç¨5.99/month annually) and unlocks ad-free experience, offline access, AI features, and unlimited content. Many useful features require the subscription.

StudySmarter also frequently pushes upgrade prompts, which can be distracting when you're trying to study.

Privacy & Data Ownership

This is where the two apps diverge most significantly.

Mayinab stores all data locally on your device. No account required. Zero tracking, zero analytics, zero data collection. Your notes and flashcards are yours, stored on your device.

StudySmarter is a cloud-only platform. It requires an account, stores all data on their servers, and collects usage analytics. StudySmarter's business model includes understanding user behavior to improve engagement, meaning your study habits are tracked and analyzed. If you value data ownership and privacy, this is a major concern.

Offline Support

Mayinab works 100% offline by default. Everything is stored locally. No internet required, ever.

StudySmarter is primarily web-based and requires an internet connection. The mobile app has some offline capabilities, but it's designed as a cloud-first tool. Full offline access requires Premium. If you need consistent offline studying, Mayinab is the reliable choice.

Note-Taking & Study Tools

Both apps offer notes and flashcards, but the approach is different.

StudySmarter provides a document-style note editor, flashcard creation, and shared study sets. It also integrates AI features (Premium) for generating summaries and flashcards from your notes. Student-uploaded content creates a shared library.

Mayinab uses a chat-style timeline with 7 distinct note types: text, flashcards, checklists, images, audio, video, and file attachments. Notes and flashcards live together chronologically. For STEM students, Markdown and LaTeX are built-in for formulas and code, something StudySmarter doesn't support.

StudySmarter has the edge in social and AI features. Mayinab has the edge in note variety, formatting power, and offline reliability.

  • Media-rich notes: 7 note types including audio, video, drawing pad, and file attachments.

  • Desktop power users: Split-view, command palette, keyboard shortcuts. Linux support.
