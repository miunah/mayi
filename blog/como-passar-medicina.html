The 3 Pillars of Medicine Vestibular Preparation

1. Biology: Your Most Important Subject

Biology carries the highest weight in Medicina vestibulares. The top areas to master:

  • Genetics: Mendel's laws, Hardy-Weinberg, DNA replication, gene expression
  • Ecology: Biomes, food webs, biogeochemical cycles, population dynamics
  • Human Physiology: Nervous system, endocrine system, circulatory system, digestion
  • Cytology: Organelles, membrane transport, mitosis, meiosis
  • Biochemistry: Krebs cycle, glycolysis, photosynthesis, protein synthesis
  • Zoology & Botany: Classification, evolution, morphology

Create detailed flashcards for each process. For example: "What are the 4 phases of mitosis and what happens in each?" This forces deep understanding, not superficial memorization.

2. Chemistry: Precision and Formulas

Chemistry for Medicina demands both conceptual understanding and calculation skills:

  • Organic Chemistry: Functional groups, IUPAC nomenclature, reaction mechanisms
  • Stoichiometry: Molar mass, yield calculations, limiting reagents
  • Electrochemistry: Galvanic cells, electrolysis, oxidation-reduction
  • Thermochemistry: Enthalpy, Hess's law, calorimetry
  • Chemical Equilibrium: Le Chatelier, Kc, Kp, pH calculations

Use Mayinab's LaTeX support to write chemical equations that render perfectly in your flashcards.

3. Physics: Understanding Over Memorization

Physics for Medicina focuses on practical applications:

  • Mechanics: Newton's laws, kinematics, energy and momentum conservation
  • Thermodynamics: Gas laws, heat transfer, thermal expansion
  • Optics: Lenses, mirrors, and vision correction (directly relevant to medicine)
  • Electricity: Circuits, Ohm's law, capacitors
  • Waves: Sound, electromagnetic spectrum, Doppler effect
