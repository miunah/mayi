Why You Need a Study Schedule

Vestibulares like FUVEST, UNICAMP, UNESP cover content from all three years of high school across dozens of subjects. Without a structured plan, students either:

  • Study only subjects they enjoy (creating dangerous gaps)
  • Jump between topics randomly (never building depth)
  • Spend too long on one area and run out of time for others
  • Forget everything from earlier months

A well-organized study schedule combined with spaced repetition solves all of these problems. Here's how to build one.

The Three Pillars of a Vestibular Schedule

1. New Content Study (Learning)

This is your primary daily activity: reading textbooks, watching lectures, and taking notes. In Mayinab, create detailed notes using Markdown and organize them by Subject and Topic.

2. Flashcard Review (Retention)

This is the secret weapon. Every day, spend 20-30 minutes reviewing due flashcards in Mayinab. The SM-2 algorithm ensures you review everything at the right time. This is what prevents you from forgetting Biology while studying Physics.

3. Practice Questions (Application)

Solving past vestibular questions teaches you how knowledge is tested. After each practice session, create flashcards for concepts you missed.

Tips for Sticking to Your Schedule

  • Start small: If you're beginning, start with 4 hours/day and build up gradually.
  • Never skip flashcard review: Even on rest days, do your 20-minute review. It's the foundation of retention.
  • Alternate subjects: Don't study the same subject for more than 2 hours straight. Switch to maintain focus.
  • Weekly evaluation: Every Sunday, check what you covered and adjust the next week's plan.
  • Rest is part of the plan: Take one full day off per week. Your brain consolidates memories during rest.
