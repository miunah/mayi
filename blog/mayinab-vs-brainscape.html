Quick Overview

Brainscape is a popular paid flashcard app that uses Confidence-Based Repetition (CBR) to optimize review scheduling. It's known for its polished UI and curated content library. Mayinab is a modern study app that combines notes and flashcards with SM-2 spaced repetition, a chat-style interface, and a commitment to being free, offline, and private.

Both focus on science-backed studying, but they differ in approach, pricing, and philosophy. Let's compare them.

Spaced Repetition: SM-2 vs Confidence-Based Repetition

The core difference is in the algorithm. Both use spaced repetition, but the underlying systems are distinct.

Mayinab uses the SM-2 algorithm, the same proven system behind Anki. Each card has an individual ease factor that adjusts based on your recall quality. Intervals grow exponentially for cards you know and shrink for difficult ones. SM-2 has decades of research backing it.

Brainscape uses Confidence-Based Repetition (CBR). After seeing a card, you rate your confidence on a 1‚Äì5 scale. Cards you're less confident about appear more frequently. It's intuitive and works well, but it's a proprietary system with less independent research than SM-2.

Both are effective for learning. SM-2 is more precise with its interval calculations, while CBR is simpler to understand. For long-term retention in high-stakes studying (medical school, bar exam, languages), SM-2's mathematical precision has a proven track record.

Pricing: Free vs Premium

This is the biggest differentiator.

Mayinab is 100% free. Every feature is included: spaced repetition, 7 note types, Markdown, LaTeX, offline mode, themes, unlimited subjects and topics. The only paid option is Vault (encrypted cloud backup) starting at $1.50/month, entirely optional.

Brainscape is one of the more expensive flashcard apps. The free tier limits you to basic features and a handful of decks. Brainscape Pro costs $9.99/month ($59.99/year) and unlocks unlimited decks, offline access, advanced analytics, and the full content library. That's $120/year on a monthly plan.

For students already paying for textbooks, courses, and living expenses, Brainscape's price tag is hard to justify when Mayinab offers comparable study tools for free.

Offline Support

Mayinab works 100% offline by default. Your data is stored locally on your device. No internet connection needed, ever.

Brainscape requires internet for the free tier. Offline access is a Pro-only feature. If you study on the go, in libraries with spotty Wi-Fi, or during commutes without signal, you'll need to pay for the privilege of offline studying.

Privacy & Data

Mayinab stores all data locally. No account required. Zero tracking, zero analytics, zero data collection. Your study data stays on your device.

Brainscape requires an account and stores everything on their cloud. Your study habits, progress data, and card content are on Brainscape's servers. They use this data to improve their product, but it means your study data isn't truly yours.

Beyond Flashcards: Notes + Flashcards

This is where Mayinab pulls far ahead. Brainscape is a flashcard-only app. There's no note-taking, no checklists, no audio/video notes, no file attachments. You create flashcards and review them, that's it.

Mayinab was built as a notes + flashcards app. You have 7 note types in a chat-style timeline: text, flashcards, checklists, images, audio, video, and files. Your lecture notes and flashcards live together. No need for a separate note-taking app.

For STEM students, Mayinab also supports Markdown and LaTeX, making it easy to write formulas, code snippets, and structured content natively. Brainscape doesn't support either.

  • Budget-conscious: Free forever vs. $9.99/month. No contest.
  • Offline studying: Works without internet, always, at no extra cost.
  • Privacy matters: Local data, no tracking, no account required.
  • STEM students: Markdown + LaTeX support built-in.
  • Proven SRS: SM-2 algorithm with decades of research backing it.
  • Desktop power users: Split-view, command palette, keyboard shortcuts.
  • Linux users: Mayinab supports Linux; Brainscape doesn't have a desktop app.

Brainscape is a polished flashcard app with great curated content. If you want pre-made, professional flashcard decks for specific certifications or exams and don't mind paying $9.99/month, Brainscape delivers a clean experience.
