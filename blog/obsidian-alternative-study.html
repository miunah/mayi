Best Obsidian Alternative for Students | Notes + Flashcards + Spaced Repetition

February 13, 2026 ¬∑ 7 min read

You Love Obsidian, But Something's Missing

You chose Obsidian because it's local-first, respects your privacy, and lets you own your data as plain Markdown files. You appreciate the graph view, bidirectional links, and the massive plugin ecosystem. As a knowledge management tool, it's hard to beat.

But as a student, you've probably noticed what's missing: there are no flashcards. There's no spaced repetition. There's no built-in way to test yourself on what you've written in your notes.

Sure, there are community plugins, but they require setup, use custom syntax, can break on updates, and offer limited review modes. You end up spending time maintaining your study system instead of actually studying.

What if there was an app that shared Obsidian's values, local-first, offline, privacy-focused, but was built specifically for studying?

Meet Mayinab: Study-First, Plugin-Free

Mayinab is a modern study app that combines notes and flashcards in a single, beautiful interface. No plugins required. No YAML frontmatter. No configuration files.

  • Flashcards built-in: type /flashcard to create a card inline with your notes
  • SM-2 spaced repetition: automatic scheduling, flip & typing modes, streak tracking
  • 7 note types: text, flashcard, checklist, image, audio, video, file
  • 100% offline: data lives on your device, always
  • Markdown + LaTeX: full support for formatted text and math equations
  • Chat-style interface: as intuitive as texting, not a code editor
  • Free on every platform: Windows, macOS, Linux, Android, iOS

Why Students Switch from Obsidian to Mayinab

1. No More Plugin Juggling

With Obsidian, getting flashcards working means installing a community plugin, learning its syntax, and hoping it stays compatible. With Mayinab, flashcards are a core feature. Type /flashcard, write front and back, done. The card gets scheduled with SM-2 automatically.

2. Notes and Flashcards Together

In Obsidian, even with plugins, flashcards live in separate tags or files from your regular notes. In Mayinab, they share the same chat-style timeline. Take a note about cellular biology, then create a flashcard testing that concept right below it. Context stays intact.

3. Zero Setup, Instant Productivity

No vaults to configure. No plugins to install. No templates to set up. Download Mayinab, create a subject (like "Biology" or "Spanish"), and start typing. You're productive in under 60 seconds.

4. Free Cloud Sync (Obsidian Charges $4/mo)

Obsidian Sync costs $4/month for end-to-end encrypted sync across devices. Mayinab includes free encrypted cloud backup (AES-256). Your data is encrypted on your device before upload. No subscription needed.

5. Built-in Drawing Pad

Mayinab includes a scribble pad for drawing diagrams, annotating images, and creating visual notes. Perfect for anatomy, circuits, math proofs, or any subject that benefits from hand-drawn illustrations. Obsidian's Canvas is designed for connecting notes visually, not for drawing.

6. Designed for Students, Not Power Users

Obsidian's flexibility is its strength, but it's also a barrier. The learning curve of vaults, plugins, CSS snippets, and Markdown syntax can overwhelm students who just want to study. Mayinab is opinionated by design: it makes the right choices for you so you can focus on learning.

What Mayinab Doesn't Replace

Let's be honest: Mayinab doesn't try to be Obsidian. If you need these features, keep using Obsidian:

  • Graph view: interactive visualization of note connections
  • Bidirectional [[backlinks]]: deep linking between notes with backlink panels
  • 1,500+ community plugins: extensibility for every possible use case
  • Plain Markdown files: portable, future-proof, readable anywhere
  • Zettelkasten methodology: atomic notes with emergent structure
  • Dataview & Templater: programmatic note querying and templates

Many students use both apps together: Obsidian for long-term knowledge management and research, Mayinab for daily study sessions and exam preparation. They complement each other perfectly.

Mayinab is the perfect Obsidian alternative if you are:

  • A student preparing for exams, certifications, or entrance tests
  • A language learner who needs vocabulary flashcards with spaced repetition
  • A medical/law student who needs to memorize large volumes of information
  • Someone who's tired of configuring plugins just to get flashcards working
  • A student who wants one app for notes AND flashcards instead of juggling Obsidian + Anki
