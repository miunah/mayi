How to Pass Vestibular de ADS (Systems Analysis): Complete Guide

What Is ADS and Why Choose It?

Analise e Desenvolvimento de Sistemas (ADS) -- Systems Analysis and Development -- is a technology degree (tecnologo) focused on practical software development. It's one of the most popular tech courses in Brazil, offered by institutions like FATEC, IFSPs, and federal universities via ENEM/SiSU.

Why ADS is an excellent choice:

  • Short duration: 2-3 years (vs. 4-5 for Computer Science)
  • Career-focused: Practical curriculum aligned with industry needs
  • High employability: Software developers are among the most demanded professionals
  • Lower entry barrier: Less competitive vestibulares compared to CS at USP/UNICAMP
  • Quality education: FATECs and IFs offer excellent education, often free

How ADS Admissions Work

ADS admissions vary by institution:

Federal universities ENEM/SiSU Math and Science scores are decisive
Private universities ENEM score or own exam Lower cutoff, scholarship opportunities

Key Subjects to Study

1. Mathematics: Your Strongest Ally

Math is critical for both the vestibular and the ADS course itself:

  • Basic Algebra: Equations, inequalities, systems of equations
  • Functions: Linear, quadratic, exponential, logarithmic
  • Statistics & Probability: Mean, median, standard deviation, combinatorics
  • Logic: Propositional logic, truth tables, set theory
  • Geometry: Areas, volumes, analytic geometry basics
  • Percentages & Financial Math: Common in FATEC and ENEM questions

2. Portuguese: Don't Underestimate It

Portuguese has significant weight, especially at FATEC:

  • Text Interpretation: Understanding arguments, inferences, and authorial intent
  • Grammar: Verb agreement, pronoun placement, punctuation
  • Text Types: Argumentative, narrative, descriptive, informative
  • ENEM Essay: Argumentative text with proposal for social intervention

3. General Knowledge & Current Events

FATEC and ENEM test broad knowledge:

  • Technology trends: AI, cybersecurity, cloud computing, data privacy
  • Brazilian politics and economy: Recent policies, social indicators
  • Environment: Climate change, sustainability, energy sources
  • Geography: Urbanization, globalization, demographics

4. Logical Reasoning

Critical thinking and logic are directly relevant to programming:

  • Sequences and patterns: Number patterns, logical sequences
  • Syllogisms: Deductive reasoning, conditional statements
  • Problem decomposition: Breaking complex problems into steps
