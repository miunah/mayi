The 6-Month Plan: Phase by Phase

Phase 1: Foundation (Months 1-2)

Goal: Cover core content of all subjects. Build your flashcard base.

  • Study 2-3 subjects per day, alternating between areas
  • Focus on high-yield topics (see priority list below)
  • Create flashcards in Mayinab for every key concept as you study
  • Begin daily flashcard review (30 min/day) -- SM-2 handles scheduling
  • Write 1 practice essay per week

Phase 2: Deepening (Months 3-4)

Goal: Cover remaining topics. Start solving past ENEM questions.

  • Continue learning new content, but reduce to ~60% of session time
  • Dedicate ~40% of session time to solving past ENEM questions by area
  • For every question you get wrong, create a flashcard explaining why
  • Increase review to 45 min/day (your card base is growing)
  • Write 2 practice essays per week
  • Take your first full-length ENEM simulation (timed, both days)

Phase 3: Intensive Review (Months 5-6)

Goal: Maximum revision. Simulations. Essay mastery.

  • No new content (unless covering specific gaps found in simulations)
  • Daily flashcard review: 45-60 min. Your SM-2 queue is your priority.
  • Solve a full ENEM simulation every 2 weeks (at least 3 total)
  • Analyze every mistake from simulations and reinforce with flashcards
  • Write 2-3 essays per week, following the ENEM competency model
  • Final week: light review only. Flashcards + rest. No cramming.

The Flashcard Strategy for ENEM

Your flashcard base is your long-term memory insurance. Here's how to build it:

  • Create immediately: Don't wait. Make cards as you study each topic.
  • One concept per card: "What are the phases of mitosis?" not "Explain all of cell biology"
  • Use LaTeX for formulas: Mayinab supports LaTeX natively -- perfect for math and physics
  • Ask "why" and "how": "Why does Brazil have more biodiversity than Europe?" beats "What is biodiversity?"
  • Card from mistakes: Every simulation question you get wrong becomes a flashcard
