The Plugin Problem: Flashcards in Obsidian

Obsidian doesn't ship with flashcards or spaced repetition. To study with Obsidian, you need to:

  1. Browse the community plugin catalog and install a flashcard plugin (like "Spaced Repetition" or "Flashcards")
  2. Learn the plugin's specific syntax (e.g., #flashcards tags, :: separators, or YAML frontmatter)
  3. Configure review settings, card types, and scheduling parameters
  4. Hope the plugin doesn't break after an Obsidian update
  5. Accept limited review modes ‚Äî most plugins only support basic flip cards

In Mayinab, you type /flashcard in the chat bar. Done. The card appears inline with your notes, gets scheduled automatically with SM-2, and offers both flip and typing modes. No configuration, no frontmatter, no plugin maintenance.

Mayinab is built for learning ‚Äî creating study material, testing yourself with active recall, and using spaced repetition to move knowledge from short-term to long-term memory.

These are different goals. Obsidian helps you organize what you know. Mayinab helps you remember what you're learning. For students preparing for exams, certifications, or language learning, the ability to remember is what matters most.

Both Are Local-First ‚Äî But Different
