Why Most Students Fail the Vestibular

Every year, millions of Brazilian students take vestibulares for universities like USP, UNICAMP, UNESP, UFMG, and others. Most fail -- not because they didn't study, but because they studied incorrectly.

The most common mistakes are:

  • Passive studying: Re-reading notes and watching videos without testing yourself
  • No review system: Studying a topic once and never revisiting it
  • Cramming: Trying to memorize everything in the last weeks before the exam
  • No organization: Jumping between subjects without a clear plan
  • Tool overload: Using five different apps that don't talk to each other

The good news is that science has clear answers about what works. Here are the 7 techniques that will actually help you pass.

1. Active Recall: Test Yourself Constantly

Active recall means closing the textbook and trying to remember the information from memory. Instead of re-reading, you quiz yourself.

Research from cognitive psychology shows that retrieval practice (actively recalling information) strengthens memory far more than passive review. Every time you successfully recall something, the neural pathway gets stronger.

How to apply: After studying a chapter, create flashcards in Mayinab and try to answer them without looking at your notes. The act of trying to remember is what builds lasting memory.

2. Spaced Repetition: Review at the Right Time

Spaced repetition is the technique of reviewing material at increasing intervals over time. Instead of reviewing everything every day, you review each concept just before you would forget it.

The SM-2 algorithm (used in Mayinab) calculates these intervals automatically. If you answer a flashcard correctly:

  • First correct answer: review in 1 day
  • Second correct answer: review in 3 days
  • Third: 7 days
  • Fourth: 21 days
  • And so on, growing exponentially

If you get it wrong, the interval resets. This ensures you spend time only on what you're about to forget.

3. Interleaving: Mix Subjects Instead of Blocking

Most students study in blocks: 3 hours of Math, then 3 hours of Biology. Research shows that interleaving -- alternating between subjects within a session -- produces better long-term retention.

When you mix subjects, your brain is forced to distinguish between different types of problems, which strengthens your ability to apply the right knowledge in the right context -- exactly what vestibulares test.

How to apply: During your daily flashcard review in Mayinab, cards from all subjects are mixed naturally. This is interleaving happening automatically.

4. Elaboration: Explain It in Your Own Words

Elaboration means connecting new information to what you already know. Instead of memorizing a definition, you explain it, give examples, and relate it to other concepts.

How to apply: In Mayinab, use notes to write explanations in your own words. Create flashcards that ask "why" and "how," not just "what."

  • Bad card: "What is mitosis?" ‚Üí "Cell division"
  • Good card: "Why is mitosis important for growth?" ‚Üí "It produces identical daughter cells, allowing tissues to grow and repair while maintaining the same DNA"

5. Concrete Examples: Make It Real

Abstract concepts are hard to remember. Attaching concrete examples to each concept makes it stick.

How to apply: When creating flashcards for History, include specific examples. Instead of "What were the causes of WWI?" use "Name 3 specific events that escalated tensions before WWI" with concrete answers like the assassination of Franz Ferdinand.

6. Dual Coding: Combine Words and Visuals

Your brain processes text and images through different channels. Combining both creates stronger memories than either alone.

How to apply: In Mayinab, add images to your notes alongside text explanations. For Biology, include diagrams of cell structures. For Geography, include maps. For Chemistry, include molecular structures.

7. Practice Testing: Simulate the Exam

Solving past vestibular questions under timed conditions is one of the best ways to prepare. It trains you to handle pressure, manage time, and identify knowledge gaps.

How to apply: After each practice exam, create flashcards in Mayinab for every question you got wrong. The SM-2 algorithm will ensure you don't make the same mistake twice.
