What Is the OAB Exam?

The Exame da Ordem dos Advogados do Brasil (OAB) is the Brazilian bar exam -- mandatory for any law graduate who wants to practice as a lawyer. It's administered by the FGV (Fundacao Getulio Vargas) and consists of two phases.

The OAB exam is known for its difficulty. Pass rates vary, but typically only 20-30% of candidates pass the first phase. The key challenge is the sheer volume of legal content that must be memorized and applied.

Exam Structure

1st Phase: Objective (Multiple Choice)

  • 80 questions across all areas of Law
  • You need at least 50% (40 correct) to pass
  • Duration: 5 hours
  • Covers: Ethics, Constitutional, Civil, Criminal, Labor, Administrative, Tax, Business, Consumer, Environmental, Human Rights, ECA, and International Law

2nd Phase: Written (Essay + Case Study)

  • 1 essay question + 4 case studies in a chosen area of law
  • Areas: Civil, Criminal, Labor, Administrative, Tax, Business, or Constitutional Law
  • You need at least 6.0 out of 10.0
  • Duration: 5 hours
  • You can consult unannoted legislation (Vade Mecum)

Flashcard Strategy for the OAB

  • Ethics: Flashcards for every article of the Estatuto da OAB (Lei 8.906/94) and Codigo de Etica.

12-Week Study Plan: 1st Phase

Weeks 1-4: Foundation

  1. Study Ethics completely -- it's the highest-yield subject
  2. Constitutional Law: Fundamental rights (Art. 5), State organization, Constitutional remedies
  3. Civil Law: General part, Obligations, Contracts
  4. Create flashcards daily in Mayinab

Weeks 5-8: Core Subjects

  1. Criminal Law: General part, Crimes against persons, Crimes against property
  2. Labor Law: Individual labor law, CLT main articles
  3. Civil Procedure: Jurisdiction, competence, procedural acts, resources
  4. Criminal Procedure: Investigation, action, evidence, resources
  5. Daily flashcard review (30 min) + new cards from study sessions

Weeks 9-12: Review + Practice

  1. Administrative Law, Tax Law, and remaining subjects
  2. Solve past OAB exams (at least 5 full exams)
  3. Create flashcards from every mistake
  4. Intensive daily review in Mayinab -- the SM-2 algorithm brings back what matters

2nd Phase Strategy

After passing the 1st phase, you have approximately 60 days to prepare for the written phase. Choose the area you're most comfortable with.

  • Study the CPC/CPP/CLT relevant to your chosen area deeply
  • Practice writing legal documents: petitions, appeals, opinions
  • Use flashcards for procedural requirements: deadlines, formalities, addressee (competence)
  • Review past 2nd phase exams in your chosen area

O que e o Exame da OAB?

O Exame da Ordem dos Advogados do Brasil (OAB) e obrigatorio para qualquer bacharel em Direito que queira exercer a advocacia. E aplicado pela FGV (Fundacao Getulio Vargas) e possui duas fases.

O exame da OAB e conhecido pela dificuldade. As taxas de aprovacao variam, mas tipicamente apenas 20-30% dos candidatos passam na primeira fase. O principal desafio e o enorme volume de conteudo juridico que precisa ser memorizado e aplicado.

Estrutura do Exame

1¬™ Fase: Objetiva (Multipla Escolha)

  • 80 questoes em todas as areas do Direito
  • Necessario acertar pelo menos 50% (40 questoes)
  • Duracao: 5 horas
  • Abrange: Etica, Constitucional, Civil, Penal, Trabalho, Administrativo, Tributario, Empresarial, Consumidor, Ambiental, Direitos Humanos, ECA e Internacional

2¬™ Fase: Escrita (Peca + Questoes Discursivas)

  • 1 peca processual + 4 questoes discursivas na area escolhida
  • Areas: Civil, Penal, Trabalho, Administrativo, Tributario, Empresarial ou Constitucional
  • Necessario nota minima de 6,0 em 10,0
  • Duracao: 5 horas
  • Permitida consulta a legislacao seca (Vade Mecum)
