The Fall of Evernote

Evernote was once the undisputed king of note-taking. Millions of users relied on it for capturing ideas, clipping web pages, and organizing knowledge. But over the years, the story changed:

  • Free tier gutted: limited to 1 device, 60 MB/month uploads, 25 MB per note
  • Prices increased: Personal plan at $15/month, Professional at $18/month
  • Performance issues: app became slower and buggier after the v10 rewrite
  • Bending Spoons acquisition: further price hikes and layoffs raised concerns
  • Cloud dependency: your notes live on their servers with no true local storage

For students, the situation is worse: Evernote never had flashcards or spaced repetition. It was always a storage tool, not a study tool. You could save information beautifully, but it offered zero help in actually learning it.
