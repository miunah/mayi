What Is ANBIMA?

ANBIMA (Brazilian Association of Financial and Capital Markets Entities) is the main self-regulatory body of Brazil's financial market. It creates and enforces codes of conduct, best practices, and professional certifications that are mandatory for anyone working with investment products at financial institutions.

If you want to work at a bank, brokerage, or investment firm in Brazil, you'll almost certainly need an ANBIMA certification. The three main ones are:

  • CPA-10: Required to work in bank branches distributing investment products
  • CPA-20: Required for professionals serving high-net-worth, corporate, and institutional clients
  • CEA: Required for investment advisors and wealth management specialists

CPA-10: Complete Content Breakdown

Module 1: Financial System (5-10%)

  • Structure of the SFN (National Financial System)
  • CMN, BACEN, CVM roles and responsibilities
  • Financial intermediaries and service providers

Module 2: Ethics, Self-Regulation & Compliance (10-15%)

  • ANBIMA codes of conduct and best practices
  • Know Your Customer (KYC) and anti-money laundering (PLD)
  • Suitability and investor profiling
  • Insider trading and market manipulation rules

Module 3: Economics (5-10%)

  • GDP, inflation (IPCA, IGP-M), SELIC, CDI
  • Monetary and fiscal policy basics
  • Exchange rates and balance of payments

Module 4: Financial Mathematics (5-10%)

  • Simple and compound interest
  • Present and future value
  • Nominal vs. real rates
  • Benchmark comparisons (CDI, SELIC, IPCA)

Module 5: Investment Products (15-25%)

  • Fixed Income: CDB, LCI, LCA, Debentures, Tesouro Direto
  • Variable Income: Stocks, ETFs, BDRs
  • FGC (Credit Guarantee Fund) coverage rules
  • Real estate investment trusts (FIIs)

Module 6: Investment Funds (20-25%)

  • Fund types: FI, FIC, Fixed Income, Multimarket, Equity, Cambial
  • Fees: management fee, performance fee, entry/exit fees
  • CVM Instruction 555 (fund regulation)
  • Chinese wall and conflict of interest policies

Module 7: Taxation (10-15%)

  • Regressive IR table for fixed income
  • Come-cotas mechanism
  • IOF on short-term investments
  • Tax-exempt products (LCI, LCA, CRI, CRA)
  • Stock market taxation (swing trade vs. day trade)

CPA-20: Additional Content

The CPA-20 includes everything from CPA-10, plus:

  • Derivatives: Options, futures, swaps, forward contracts
  • Risk Management: VaR, stress testing, duration, convexity
  • Portfolio Theory: CAPM, efficient frontier, Markowitz, Sharpe ratio
  • International Investments: BDRs, offshore funds, foreign currency exposure
  • Private Banking: Succession planning, tax optimization, family offices
  • Credit Products: FIDC, CRI, CRA, securitization

Common Mistakes to Avoid

  • Studying only theory: ANBIMA exams are practical. Focus on application, not just definitions.
  • Ignoring ethics/compliance: This module is "easy points" but many candidates skip it.
  • Not doing mock exams: You need to practice the exam format and time management.
  • Cramming instead of spacing: Spaced repetition with SM-2 beats last-minute cramming every time.

Key Numbers to Memorize

  • FGC limit: Check the current FGC limit per CPF per institution on the FGC website
  • COAF reporting: Cash transactions above the current threshold
  • IR regressive table: 22.5% / 20% / 17.5% / 15%
  • Come-cotas: May and November (last business day)
  • IOF: Decreasing daily rate, zero after 30 days
  • Day trade IR: 20% (vs. 15% swing trade for stocks)
