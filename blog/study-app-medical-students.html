Why Medical Students Need Spaced Repetition

Medical school is unique. You're expected to memorize an enormous volume of factual knowledge, anatomy, biochemistry, pharmacology, pathology, microbiology, and retain it across years of training. Re-reading lecture notes is not enough.

Spaced repetition is the most effective technique for this challenge. By reviewing flashcards at algorithmically calculated intervals, the SM-2 system ensures you spend your study time where it matters most: on material you're about to forget.

That's why Anki has been the go-to tool for medical students for years. But Anki has problems. Mayinab offers a modern alternative that keeps the best part (SM-2) while solving Anki's biggest pain points.

The Anki Problem in Medical School

Ask any medical student about Anki and you'll hear two things: "it's essential" and "it's painful." The common frustrations:

  • Complex setup: Configuring note types, card types, and deck options takes hours before you can start studying.
  • Dated interface: The UI hasn't meaningfully changed since the 2000s. Students used to sleek modern apps find it jarring.
  • No note-taking: Anki only does flashcards. You still need Notion, OneNote, or Google Docs for lecture notes, creating a fragmented workflow.
  • Plugin dependency: Image occlusion, better editors, and custom styling require add-ons that can break with updates.
  • iOS cost: AnkiMobile is $24.99, a real expense for students.

Many medical students use Anki despite these issues, not because of them. Mayinab gives you the same SM-2 algorithm without the friction.

How Mayinab Works for Medical Students

This structure mirrors how medical education is organized, by organ systems in preclinical years and by clinical clerkships later.

  • Draw pathways, cycles, and clinical algorithms

SM-2 Spaced Repetition for High-Volume Decks

Medical students often accumulate thousands of flashcards across preclinical years. SM-2 handles this volume efficiently:

  • Cards you know well are reviewed less frequently (weeks to months apart)
  • Difficult cards return more often (days apart)
  • New cards are introduced at a controlled pace
  • Daily review sessions stay manageable (30-60 minutes for 100-200 cards)

The key is to review every day. With SM-2, you never have 500 cards due at once; the algorithm spreads them out naturally.

Medical Study Workflow with Mayinab

  1. Before class: Skim the textbook chapter. Create a topic in Mayinab for the lecture's subject.
  2. During class: Take notes in real time. Use Markdown for formatting, LaTeX for formulas.
  3. After each concept: Create a flashcard inline with /flashcard. Focus on high-yield facts.
  4. Same day: Review new cards in the evening. SM-2 will show them again tomorrow.
  5. Daily routine: 30 minutes of spaced repetition review. Clear your due cards.
  6. Before boards: Your accumulated knowledge is already in long-term memory. Focus on practice questions instead of cramming.
