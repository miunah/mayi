Why Students Need More Than Samsung Notes

Samsung Notes is designed for capturing information: handwritten notes in class, quick text memos, voice recordings. It does this well, especially with the S Pen on Galaxy Tab.

But capturing information is only half the learning process. The other half is retrieval practice and spaced repetition, the two most effective study techniques according to cognitive science. You need to actively test yourself on what you've written, at scientifically optimal intervals.

Samsung Notes has no way to do this. You can't create flashcards from your notes. You can't schedule reviews. You can't track what you've mastered and what you still need to study. Students end up re-reading their notes passively, one of the least effective study methods.

Mayinab: Notes That Actually Help You Remember

In Mayinab, your study flow is continuous. Take a text note about a topic, then type /flashcard right below it to create a card. The flashcard lives alongside your notes in a chat-style timeline. No switching apps, no copy-pasting.

When it's time to review, Mayinab's built-in SM-2 spaced repetition algorithm schedules your cards automatically. You get flip mode or typing mode, streak tracking, and daily reminders. Your learning is science-based, not just note storage.

This is the fundamental difference: Samsung Notes stores information. Mayinab helps you learn it.
