Why People Look for Anki Alternatives

Anki is a powerful tool with a loyal community. But it has well-known pain points that push many students to look elsewhere:

  • Outdated interface: Anki's UI hasn't had a meaningful visual update in years. The Qt-based desktop interface feels like software from 2005.
  • Steep learning curve: Note types, card types, templates, fields, deck options. Anki has incredible flexibility, but beginners often feel overwhelmed.
  • No note-taking: Anki only does flashcards. You still need a separate app (Notion, OneNote, Obsidian) for your notes, creating a fragmented workflow.
  • Plugin dependency: Many essential features (image occlusion, better editor, custom styling) require community add-ons that can break with updates.
  • Mobile cost: AnkiDroid is free on Android, but AnkiMobile costs $24.99 on iOS.
