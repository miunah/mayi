What Is the Policia Federal?

The Policia Federal (PF) is Brazil's national law enforcement agency, equivalent to the FBI in the United States. It's responsible for federal crimes, drug trafficking, cybercrime, border security, immigration, and investigating crimes against the federal government.

A career at the PF offers:

  • Prestige: One of the most respected law enforcement agencies in Latin America
  • High salary: Among the highest in Brazilian law enforcement (check the latest edital for updated values)
  • Job stability: Lifetime tenure after probation period
  • Benefits: Health insurance, housing allowance, hazard pay, pension
  • Career growth: Promotions and specialization in major operations
  • Impact: Fight organized crime, corruption, and protect national borders

Subject-by-Subject Strategy

1. Criminal Law (Direito Penal)

The most important subject for PF:

  • General part: Crime theory, typicality, illegality, culpability
  • Penalties: Types, application, aggravating/mitigating factors, dosimetry
  • Attempt & participation: Co-authorship, instigation, complicity
  • Extinction of punishability: Prescription, amnesty, pardon
  • Specific crimes: Crimes against persons, property, public administration, public faith
  • Crimes against public administration: Corruption, embezzlement (peculato), malfeasance

2. Criminal Procedure (Processual Penal)

  • Police inquiry (Inquerito Policial): Characteristics, procedures, deadlines
  • Criminal action: Public vs. private, conditions, deadlines
  • Arrest: Flagrante, preventive, temporary -- requirements and procedures
  • Evidence: Types, lawfulness, chain of custody
  • Jurisdiction & competence: Federal vs. state, territorial
  • Habeas corpus: Requirements, when applicable

3. Special Legislation

  • Drug Law (Lei 11.343/06): Use vs. trafficking, penalties, investigation
  • Organized Crime (Lei 12.850/13): Definition, investigation methods, plea bargaining (delacao premiada)
  • Firearms Statute (Estatuto do Desarmamento): Possession, carrying, registration
  • Torture Law (Lei 9.455/97): Definition, penalties
  • Racism Law (Lei 7.716/89): Crimes of racial prejudice
  • Money Laundering (Lei 9.613/98): Phases, COAF, reporting obligations
  • Abuse of Authority (Lei 13.869/19): New law, key provisions

4. Constitutional Law

  • Fundamental rights (Art. 5): All key incisos, especially those related to criminal procedure
  • Public security (Art. 144): PF's constitutional role and jurisdiction
  • State organization: Separation of powers, federalism
  • Constitutional remedies: Habeas corpus, mandado de seguranca, habeas data

5. Administrative Law

  • Principles (LIMPE): Legality, Impersonality, Morality, Publicity, Efficiency
  • Administrative acts: Elements, attributes, invalidation, revocation
  • Public servants regime: Lei 8.112/90 -- rights, duties, disciplinary process
  • Administrative improbity: Lei 8.429/92 (updated by Lei 14.230/21)

6. Portuguese Language

  • Text interpretation: CESPE loves inference and implicit meaning questions
  • Grammar judging: Identify if rewrites maintain meaning and correctness
  • Concordancia, regencia, crase: Classic CESPE traps
  • Pronoun placement: Proclise, mesoclise, enclise rules

CESPE/CEBRASPE Strategy

The CESPE exam has a unique format that requires specific strategy:

  • Right/Wrong (Certo/Errado): Each statement is either right or wrong. Wrong answers deduct points.
  • Leave blank when unsure: If you're not at least 70% confident, skip. A blank costs 0; a wrong answer costs -1.
  • Read word by word: CESPE changes ONE word to make a statement wrong. "Exclusive" instead of "preferential", "always" instead of "generally".
  • Absolute words are traps: "always", "never", "only", "exclusively" often make statements wrong.
  • Half-right = wrong: If half the statement is right but the other half is wrong, the whole thing is wrong.

  • Front: "What are the 3 phases of money laundering (Lei 9.613/98)?"
  • Back: "1) Placement (colocacao): Introducing illicit money into the financial system. 2) Layering (ocultacao): Creating layers of transactions to obscure the trail. 3) Integration (integracao): Reinserting cleaned money into the legitimate economy.   • Front: "According to Art. 144 CF/88, what are the PF's exclusive functions?"
  • Back: "I - Investigate criminal offenses against the political and social order or to the detriment of assets, services, and interests of the Union; II - Prevent and repress trafficking of drugs and smuggling; III - Exercise maritime, airport, and border police functions; IV - Exercise, exclusively, the functions of federal judicial police."

Physical Fitness Test (TAF) Preparation

The TAF is eliminatory. Common requirements:

  • Pull-ups (barra fixa): 3+ (men), flexed-arm hang 20s+ (women)
  • Sit-ups (abdominais): 30+ in 1 minute
  • Push-ups (flexoes): 25+ (men), 15+ (women)
  • 50m sprint: Under 8s (men), under 9s (women)
  • Cooper test (12-min run): 2,000m+ (men), 1,600m+ (women)

Tip: Start physical preparation 6+ months before the TAF. Combine cardio and strength training 4-5x/week alongside study.
