Google Keep: Great for Groceries, Not for Studying

Google Keep is popular because it's simple, free, and pre-installed on most Android phones. It's perfect for jotting down a grocery list, setting a reminder, or saving a quick thought.

But Google Keep was never designed for studying. It has:

  • No flashcards
  • No spaced repetition
  • No Markdown or LaTeX
  • No note organization beyond labels and colors
  • No long-form note support (notes get unwieldy after a few paragraphs)
  • No desktop app (web only on computers)

Students who try to use Google Keep for studying end up with a chaotic collection of short notes that are impossible to review, impossible to organize, and impossible to learn from.
