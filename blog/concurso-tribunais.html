Why Work at a Court (Tribunal)?

Courts (Tribunais) are among the highest-paying and most stable public service careers in Brazil. The Judiciary has its own budget and consistently offers competitive salaries, excellent benefits, and work-life balance.

  • High salaries: Among the best in Brazilian public service (check the latest edital for updated values)
  • Job stability: Lifetime tenure after 3-year probation
  • Strong benefits: Health/dental insurance, meal allowance, education support, pension
  • 30-35h workweek: Better work-life balance than most sectors
  • Career growth: Internal promotions, substitution of judges/directors
  • Frequent exams: New concursos every 1-3 years across dozens of courts

Subject-by-Subject Strategy

1. Constitutional Law

  • Fundamental rights (Art. 5): Essential incisos, especially due process, access to justice
  • Organization of the Judiciary (Arts. 92-126): Court structure, competences, guarantees
  • Public administration principles (Art. 37): LIMPE, public servants
  • Constitutional remedies: Habeas corpus, mandado de seguranca, acao popular
  • Fundamental social rights: Labor rights (for TRT)

2. Administrative Law

  • Administrative acts: Elements (COFIFOMOB), attributes, invalidation
  • Public servants (Lei 8.112/90): Rights, duties, disciplinary sanctions
  • Bidding and contracts (Lei 14.133/21): Modalities, exemptions, contract clauses
  • Administrative improbity (Lei 8.429/92): Types of improbity, penalties
  • Administrative process (Lei 9.784/99): Principles, phases, appeals

3. Civil Procedural Law (CPC - for TJ and TRF)

  • Principles: Adversarial principle, due process, cooperation
  • Jurisdiction and competence: Types, modification, conflicts
  • Parties: Capacity, representation, intervention of third parties
  • Acts of process: Deadlines, nullities, electronic process
  • Ordinary procedure: Petition, answer, evidence, judgment
  • Appeals: Types, deadlines, effects, requirements

4. Labor Law & Process (for TRT)

  • CLT fundamentals: Employment contract, termination, FGTS
  • Working hours: Limits, overtime, night work, breaks
  • Labor procedure: Dissidios, audiencia, recursos
  • Unions: Organization, collective bargaining, strikes

5. Portuguese Language

  • Text interpretation: The highest-weight subject after law
  • Grammar: Concordancia, regencia, crase, verb tenses
  • Text rewriting: Very common in FCC exams
  • Official writing: Oficio, despacho, certidao (judicial acts)
