The 5 ENEM Essay Competencies

Competency 1: Command of Formal Written Portuguese (200 pts)

This evaluates your grammar, spelling, punctuation, and register. You must write in formal standard Portuguese (norma culta).

  • No slang, colloquialisms, or internet language
  • Correct verb agreement, pronoun placement, punctuation
  • Proper use of accents and spelling rules

How to improve: Create flashcards for the most common grammar mistakes in ENEM essays: verb agreement with collective nouns, use of "onde" vs "em que", comma rules, and crase usage. Review daily with SM-2 in Mayinab.

Competency 2: Understanding the Theme (200 pts)

You must demonstrate that you understood the prompt and are addressing the specific theme proposed -- not a tangential topic. Going off-topic results in a zero.

  • Read all support texts carefully
  • Identify the specific problem the theme addresses
  • Your thesis must directly respond to the theme
  • Stay on-topic throughout all paragraphs

Competency 3: Argumentation with Sociocultural Repertoire (200 pts)

This is where most students struggle. You need to support your arguments with external references: philosophers, laws, statistics, historical events, literary works, or films.

The repertoire must be productive -- not just mentioned, but integrated into your argument:

  • Bad: "As the philosopher Kant said, we should act morally." (citation without connection)
  • Good: "According to Kant's categorical imperative, every person must be treated as an end in themselves, never as a means. In the context of [theme], this principle is violated when..." (integrated into argument)

Competency 5: Intervention Proposal (200 pts)

The ENEM essay requires a detailed intervention proposal in the conclusion. This must include 5 elements:

  1. Agent: Who will act? (Government, Ministry of Education, NGOs, media, schools...)
  2. Action: What will be done? (Create a program, implement a policy, launch a campaign...)
  3. Means: How? (Through legislation, public investment, partnerships, technology...)
  4. Purpose: Why? What's the goal? (In order to reduce, to promote, to guarantee...)
  5. Detail: An extra specification of any element above

Template: "Therefore, [AGENT] must [ACTION] by means of [MEANS], with the purpose of [PURPOSE], [DETAIL]."

How to prepare: Create flashcards with intervention templates for common themes. Practice writing complete proposals until the 5-element structure becomes automatic.

The Perfect ENEM Essay Structure

Introduction (7-10 lines)

  1. Hook: Start with a relevant reference (philosopher, fact, historical event)
  2. Contextualization: Connect the reference to the theme
  3. Thesis: State your position clearly, previewing your 2 arguments

Example pattern: "[Reference]. In the Brazilian context, [connection to theme]. Nesse sentido, [thesis: problem persists due to Argument 1 and Argument 2]."

Development 1 (8-12 lines)

  1. Topic sentence: Introduce Argument 1
  2. Repertoire: Support with external reference
  3. Analysis: Explain how the reference connects to the theme
  4. Closing: Reinforce the point and transition

Development 2 (8-12 lines)

Same structure as Development 1, but with Argument 2 and a different repertoire reference.

Conclusion (7-10 lines)

  1. Connective: "Diante do exposto..." / "√Ä luz do exposto..."
  2. Thesis summary: Brief restatement of the problem
  3. Intervention proposal: Agent + Action + Means + Purpose + Detail
