The Note-and-Forget Trap

Here's a pattern every student knows: spend hours taking beautiful, organized notes in Notion, color-coded, with toggles, databases, and templates. Then never look at them again until the night before the exam.

This is the "note-and-forget" trap. Notion makes it easy to store information, but it does nothing to help you remember it. Without active recall and spaced repetition, your notes are just a digital notebook collecting dust.

Mayinab breaks this cycle. When you take notes, you also create flashcards. When it's time to study, the SM-2 algorithm tells you exactly which cards to review and when. Your knowledge is actively reinforced, not passively stored.

Why Students Are Switching from Notion

  • No flashcards in Notion: you need Anki, Quizlet, or workarounds
  • No spaced repetition: no way to schedule reviews scientifically
  • Limited offline: can't study without internet reliably
  • Complexity overkill: databases, relations, and formulas are great for work, not for studying organic chemistry
  • Cloud dependency: your study notes live on someone else's server
  • Free tier limits: file upload limits, block limits, guest restrictions

Mayinab: The Study App Notion Should Have Been

Imagine if Notion had flashcards. And spaced repetition. And worked offline. And was free with no limits. That's Mayinab.

Instead of building a general productivity tool and hoping students adapt, Mayinab was designed specifically for learning. Every feature exists to help you study more effectively:

  • /flashcard: create cards inline with your notes
  • /checklist: track study goals and tasks
  • SM-2 scheduling: review at optimal intervals
  • Streak tracking: stay consistent with daily study
  • Split-view: read notes on one side, review cards on the other
  • Command palette (Ctrl+K): find any subject or topic instantly
  • Drawing pad: sketch diagrams, annotate images

Who Should Stay with Notion?

Notion is still the better choice for:

  • Team collaboration and shared workspaces
  • Project management with Kanban boards
  • Company wikis and documentation
  • Complex databases with formulas and relations

If your primary goal is studying and retaining knowledge, Mayinab is the right tool. If your primary goal is organizing team projects, Notion is the right tool. Many students use both: Notion for group projects, Mayinab for personal study.
